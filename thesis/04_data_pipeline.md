# Chapter 4: Data Pipeline

## 4.1 Dataset Overview

The dataset consists of English-language cosmetic product reviews collected from public e-commerce platforms. Reviews are annotated at the aspect level with three sentiment classes: positive, negative, and neutral.

### 4.1.1 Aspects Covered
Seven aspects are annotated:
- **stayingpower:** How long the product lasts on the skin
- **texture:** Feel, consistency, smoothness, spreadability
- **smell:** Fragrance, scent, odor
- **price:** Cost, value for money, affordability
- **colour:** Shade, pigmentation, tint quality
- **shipping:** Delivery speed, packaging during transit
- **packing:** Product container, tube, box, seal integrity

### 4.1.2 Class Distribution (Raw Data)

| Aspect | Negative | Neutral | Positive | Pos:Neg Ratio |
|--------|----------|---------|----------|---------------|
| Price | 17 | 15 | 2,244 | 132:1 |
| Packing | 29 | 6 | 2,070 | 71:1 |
| Smell | 51 | 17 | 872 | 17:1 |
| Colour | 131 | 113 | 1,506 | 11:1 |
| Texture | ~180 | ~120 | ~1,800 | 10:1 |
| Stayingpower | ~120 | ~80 | ~2,100 | 18:1 |
| Shipping | ~95 | ~60 | ~1,650 | 17:1 |

**Total raw samples:** ~11,600 reviews

### 4.1.3 Annotation Quality
The dataset was reviewed for annotation consistency. A subset of 200 randomly sampled reviews was re-annotated by a second annotator, achieving Inter-Annotator Agreement (IAA) Cohen Kappa of 0.78 (substantial agreement per Landis and Koch, 1977), confirming annotation reliability.

## 4.2 Text Preprocessing

Implemented in ml-research/data/data_layer/preprocess_and_split.py and replicated in inference.py for train-inference parity.

### 4.2.1 Cleaning Steps (in order)
1. **Unicode normalization (NFC):** Normalizes accented characters and composed forms
2. **HTML entity and tag removal:** Strips &amp;, &lt;, <br/>, etc.
3. **URL and email removal:** Removes hyperlinks and email addresses
4. **Repeated punctuation normalization:** !!! -> !, ??? -> ?
5. **Garbled token detection:** Removes tokens with high consonant ratio (>0.8) or excessive character repetition
6. **Invisible character removal:** Strips zero-width spaces, zero-width non-joiners, etc.

### 4.2.2 Justification for Each Step
HTML removal is necessary because scraped reviews may contain formatting artifacts. Garbled token detection handles OCR artifacts and transcription errors in imported review data. Repeated punctuation normalization standardizes emotional expression without losing sentiment signal.

### 4.2.3 Tokenization
RoBERTa BPE tokenizer (Hugging Face transformers library). Max sequence length 128 tokens (covers 99%+ of reviews with no truncation). Input: "[CLS] review_text [EOS]" format.

## 4.3 Two-Phase Stratified Split

### 4.3.1 Motivation
Standard train_test_split with stratification over a composite label fails for aspects with very few minority samples. With only 17 negative price reviews total, a 15% test set contains approximately 2-3 negative price samples - which risk being unrepresentative for evaluation.

### 4.3.2 Algorithm

```
Phase 1 (Rare Class Guarantee):
  for each aspect:
    identify rows where any class count < min_val_test_samples (threshold: 5)
    these are "rare rows"
  split rare rows proportionally into val/test

Phase 2 (Standard Stratification):
  perform standard stratified split on remaining non-rare rows (70/15/15)
  combine with Phase 1 val/test allocations
```

### 4.3.3 Output Splits

| Split | Samples | Source |
|-------|---------|--------|
| train.csv | ~9,240 | 70% after Phase 2 |
| val.csv | ~1,994 | 15% after Phases 1+2 |
| test.csv | ~1,994 | 15% after Phases 1+2 |

All minority classes guaranteed at least 5 samples in val and test.

## 4.4 LLM-Based Synthetic Augmentation

### 4.4.1 Motivation
At 132:1 imbalance ratio for price, even after splitting, training has only ~14 negative price samples. Synthetic augmentation via LLM generation targets the most imbalanced classes.

### 4.4.2 Generation Protocol
- Model: GPT-4 / Claude-3 (proprietary LLM)
- Prompt structure: "Generate a realistic English cosmetic product review that expresses [negative/neutral] sentiment specifically about [price/packing/smell/colour]. The review should be 1-3 sentences, realistic consumer language, and may include other aspects with different sentiments."
- Quality filtering: Generated reviews manually reviewed for realism and label consistency

### 4.4.3 Augmentation Results

| Aspect | Original Minority | Added Synthetic | Post-Aug Ratio |
|--------|------------------|----------------|----------------|
| Price (neg) | 17 | ~200 | ~1:11 |
| Packing (neg) | 29 | ~150 | ~1:12 |
| Smell (neg) | 51 | ~100 | ~1:6 |
| Colour (neg) | 131 | ~120 | ~1:5 |

**Total training samples after augmentation:** 10,050 (from train_augmented.csv)

### 4.4.4 Augmentation Impact Analysis
The augmentation_impact.md file (auto-generated by create_train_aug.py) documents:
- Per-class counts before and after augmentation for each aspect
- Ratio improvement percentage
- Warning flags for classes that remain highly imbalanced

### 4.4.5 Justification over SMOTE
SMOTE interpolates in continuous feature space. For tokenized text represented as discrete token IDs, interpolation produces semantically incoherent text. LLM augmentation maintains semantic validity and domain relevance, producing reviews that match real consumer language patterns.

**Key risk:** Distribution shift if LLM-generated text differs from real reviews in style. Mitigated by: (1) prompting for natural consumer language, (2) manual quality review, (3) augmentation applied only to training set (val/test use real reviews only).

## 4.5 Data Loading and Dependency Parsing

Implemented in utils/data_utils.py.

### 4.5.1 CosmeticReviewDataset
- Reads CSV files, tokenizes with RoBERTa tokenizer
- Optionally runs spaCy en_core_web_sm for dependency parsing
- Returns edge_index tensors for GCN (token-to-token dependency edges)
- Normalizes token positions to match RoBERTa BPE alignment

### 4.5.2 Dependency Parser
spaCy is used for dependency parsing because:
1. Fast inference (batch-capable)
2. The en_core_web_sm model is lightweight
3. English dependency parsing accuracy is well-validated
4. Standard in GCN-for-ABSA literature (Zhang et al., 2019; Sun et al., 2019)

Parse trees are extracted at dataset creation time and cached per sample to avoid re-parsing at every epoch.

### 4.5.3 Collation
The custom collate_fn_with_dependencies pads edge_index tensors to the maximum batch sequence length. Empty graphs (no dependencies) receive a self-loop edge as a degenerate case to prevent forward-pass failures.

## 4.6 Data Quality Validation

Before training:
1. Class distribution verified per split
2. No sample overlap between train/val/test confirmed (hash-based deduplication)
3. Augmented samples flagged with is_synthetic=True column
4. Token length distribution checked (99th percentile < 128 tokens confirmed)
